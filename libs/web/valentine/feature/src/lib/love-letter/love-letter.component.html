My Dearest Victoria,

I've been thinking about us lately — the whole wild, ridiculous, beautiful story of us. A Tinder match in the summer of 2015. A boy from Birmingham. A girl from Augusta. Three years of Snapchat conversations, near-misses, and excuses that were really just nerves neither of us would admit to.

You were this mystery girl who was absolutely stunning, hilarious, and seemed to like everything I liked (though I'm now fairly certain you were just stalking my interests online). You made it so obvious you were into me while I played it cool — badly. Every time we were supposed to meet, one of us would find a "reasonable" excuse. I think we were both terrified that the real thing might be even better than what we'd built up in our heads.

September 2018. Northside Tavern. Seven beers deep, playing pool with Seth, and you texted asking what I was doing. The liquid courage kicked in just enough to say "I'm coming." Seth thought you were a 40-year-old man living in his mom's basement. I'm so glad you weren't. He drove me to your apartment in Midtown, my heart pounding out of my chest as I rode that elevator to the 10th floor, hoping it would break down halfway up. It didn't. I walked to the end of that hallway, heard your music blaring, and then your voice for the very first time: "It's unlocked!!!"

That door opened and there you were — arms wide, biggest smile I'd ever seen, walking toward me like we'd known each other forever. Then you poured us Fireball shots and I immediately thought: that's my girl. (OK, I actually thought you were either nervous or an alcoholic. But also: that's my girl.)

You were unbelievably stunning. Drop dead gorgeous. Absolutely tiny — I felt like I could fit you in my pocket. 5 foot even, though you insist on that extra inch. Your smile was infectious. The conversation was effortless. And the next morning, my stupid ass texted YOU instead of Seth: "it went amazing — probably spent a million dollars." Your response? "I can Venmo you if you want." The horror.

Then there was Vietnam. You texted that you loved me completely out of the blue. I panicked, sweated for three hours, and responded with about 30 multicolored heart emojis. You were so mad. But I was falling so hard I didn't know what to do with myself.

And then that promotion dinner — you planned the most thoughtful evening at the nicest steakhouse in Atlanta to celebrate me. And halfway through, my body decided to betray me in the most violent, disgusting way possible. I turned on your shower to cover the sounds. I took the elevator down ten flights to use the lobby bathroom. And at 3 AM, sweet Victoria loaded me into the car, drove to get Gatorade, Pepto-Bismol, and Pedialyte, and nursed me back to health. That was the moment I knew. Not when you looked stunning. Not when you laughed at my jokes. When you showed up at 3 AM with Pedialyte. That's love.

Fast forward through a new career as a flight attendant, a cross-country move to Philly (where I drove a U-Haul 14 hours through the night while you slept in the passenger seat — after swearing you wouldn't), and suddenly I'm on one knee in Rittenhouse Park in December 2023, asking you to be my wife. We learned how to love each other better.

I built this because a Hallmark card could never hold everything I feel for you. You are ferociously loyal, impossibly thoughtful, stronger than anyone I know, and the most beautiful person I have ever seen in my life. You are authentically, unapologetically yourself — and you're not changing for anyone. I wouldn't want you to.

You are my Fireball-shot-pouring, Pedialyte-fetching, TikTok-doom-scrolling, iced-matcha-drinking, Justin-Bieber-loving, 5-foot-nothing angel. And I can't believe you're mine.

I love you more than words (or code) could ever express.

Forever yours,
